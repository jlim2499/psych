A community psychiatrist is keen to know how long patients with depression continue to be prescribed antidepressants by their general practitioner. They conduct a study and discover that the median duration of a prescription is 6 months with an interquartile range of 3 to 12 months. Based on these results, which of the following is true?

25% of patients are prescribed antidepressants for less than 3 months

The correct answer is '25% of patients are prescribed antidepressants for less than 3 months'. This statement is true because the interquartile range, which represents the middle 50% of data points in a dataset, is from 3 to 12 months. This means that the lower quartile (the 25th percentile) is at 3 months and thus, 25% of patients are prescribed antidepressants for less than this value.

The statement 'The lower quartile for the data set is 6 months' is incorrect. The lower quartile, also known as Q1 or the first quartile, refers to the middle number between the smallest number in a dataset and its median. In this case, it's mentioned that the interquartile range lies between 3 to 12 months which implies that the lower quartile (Q1) is at 3 months not at 6 months.

'75% of patients are prescribed antidepressants for 3 to 12 months' is also incorrect. The interquartile range represents only the middle half (or approximately 50%) of all data points in a dataset, not three-quarters or 75%. Therefore, it cannot be stated with certainty that three-quarters of all patients have been prescribed antidepressants within this time frame.

The option 'The upper quartile for the data set is 15 months' is incorrect as well. The upper quartile, or Q3, marks off the lowest quarter of data from the highest quarter in a dataset. Here it's given as part of an interquartile range which spans from 3 to 12 months implying that Q3 or upper quartile occurs at month twelve not fifteen.

Finally, saying '50% of patients are prescribed antidepressants for more than 12 months' is also incorrect. The median, which is the middle value that separates the higher half from the lower half of a dataset, is given as 6 months. This means that 50% of patients are prescribed antidepressants for less than or equal to 6 months and not more than 12 months.
