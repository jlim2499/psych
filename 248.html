Which of the following is one of Nolan's three fundamental questions for achieving improvement?

How will we know that a change is an improvement?

Previous Exam Question

The correct answer is How will we know that a change is an improvement?. This is one of Nolan's three fundamental questions for achieving improvement. It emphasises the importance of measurement to determine whether a change has resulted in an improvement. In a clinical context, this may involve tracking specific metrics such as patient satisfaction scores, reduced waiting times, or improvements in symptom severity. Understanding how to measure improvement ensures that efforts are data-driven and outcomes-focused.
