The correct answer is Construct validity involves the comprehensive evaluation of the questionnaire through correlation, theoretical justification, and empirical evidence, ensuring it measures the construct of anxiety and not other variables. Construct validity is the overarching term that refers to how well a test or tool measures the theoretical psychological construct it claims to assess. In this case, it is not enough to simply correlate with other measures — it must also be grounded in a theoretical framework, distinguish the target construct from others (discriminant validity), and show associations with relevant variables (convergent validity). Clinically, this ensures that a questionnaire assessing anxiety is not inadvertently measuring related but distinct phenomena like depression or general distress. For example, a well-validated anxiety scale should correlate strongly with other established anxiety measures, but not with unrelated constructs like psychotic symptoms.
