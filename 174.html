A study looking at glucose levels in a population of people taking antipsychotics finds a mean value of 7mmol/L. The standard deviation of the sample is 6mmol/L and the sample size is 9. The standard error of the mean is 2mmol/L. Which of the following is the closest approximation for the correct range for the 95% confidence interval?

3-11 mmol/L

The correct answer is 3-11 mmol/L. To determine the 95% confidence interval for the mean, we use the following formula:

Confidence Interval = μ ± (Z × SE)

Where:

μ is the sample mean
Z is the Z-score for a 95% confidence interval (which is 1.96)
SE is the standard error of the mean
Given:

μ = 7 mmol/L
SE = 2 mmol/L
The 95% confidence interval is calculated as:

7 ± (1.96 × 2)

7 ± 3.92

Lower bound = 7 - 3.92 = 3.08

Upper bound = 7 + 3.92 = 10.92

Rounding to the nearest whole number, the range is approximately 3 to 11 mmol/L.
