Which of the following influences the statistical power of a study?

Sample size

The correct answer is Sample size. Statistical power, in the context of a study, refers to the probability that the study will detect an effect or difference if there is one to be detected. This power is directly influenced by the sample size, with larger samples generally providing greater statistical power. This is because larger samples are more likely to accurately represent the population and therefore more likely to detect true effects.

The standard error of the mean does not directly influence statistical power. It is a measure of how spread out values are around the mean, but it doesn't affect the ability of a study to detect an effect. However, it could indirectly impact statistical power as smaller standard errors may lead to smaller confidence intervals and potentially more statistically significant results.

Observation bias, while an important consideration in research design and analysis, does not directly influence statistical power either. Observation bias can introduce systematic errors into a study's findings but does not inherently alter its ability to detect an effect.

Similarly, Confounding factors can distort the relationship between variables within a study and may lead to inaccurate conclusions being drawn; however, they do not directly impact on the statistical power of a study.

Finally, Cronbach's alpha is a measure of internal consistency or reliability in psychometric testing. While it's crucial for ensuring that scales or tests used in research are reliable and accurate, it doesn't directly influence the statistical power of a study.

In conclusion, whilst all these factors play key roles in conducting robust and reliable research studies only sample size influences the statistical power of a study directly.
