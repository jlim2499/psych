A study of 30 patients with depression compares the effectiveness of new antidepressant medication with standard treatment. 80% of the new treatment group achieved remission at 6 weeks, compared with only 40% of the standard treatment group.
What is the number needed to treat for the new treatment?


3

NNT = 1 / ARR
Explanation for the Correct Answer:

The Absolute Risk Reduction (ARR) is the difference in the success rates between the new treatment and the standard treatment, which is 40% (0.40 as a decimal). To calculate the NNT, we take the inverse of the ARR, giving us 2.5, which we round up to the nearest whole number for practical application in a clinical setting.

NNT = 1 / ARR

ARR = CER - EER

CER = 0.4
EER = 0.8

ARR = CER - EER
= 0.4 -0.8
= 0.4

NNT = 1 / ARR
= 1 / 0.4
= 2.5

This value of 2.5 must be rounded up to 3 as the NNT must be a whole number (we don't count half a human).
