The Royal College of Psychiatrists conducts a study on a random sample of trainees to investigate how many complaints are made against trainees whilst in training. The results are normally distributed with a mean of 5 and a standard deviation of 2. What proportion of the sample got 3 or more complaints?

85%

The correct answer is 85%. To determine the proportion of the sample that received 3 or more complaints, we need to consider the properties of the normal distribution. Given a mean of 5 and a standard deviation of 2, we know the following:

Approximately 68.3% of values lie within one standard deviation of the mean (between 3 and 7 complaints in this case).

This means that 34.15% of values lie above the mean (5 complaints) and below one standard deviation above the mean (7 complaints), and another 34.15% lie below the mean and above one standard deviation below the mean (3 complaints).

The remaining 31.7% of values lie outside this range, equally split with 15.85% below 3 complaints and 15.85% above 7 complaints.

Since we are only interested in the proportion of values that lie above 3 complaints, we combine the 68.3% (within the range of 3 to 7 complaints) and the 15.85% (above 7 complaints), giving us:

68.3% + 15.85% = 84.15%, which approximates to 85%.
