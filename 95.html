Which of the following examples demonstrates an indirect association?

Socioeconomic status influencing health outcomes through access to healthcare

The correct answer is Socioeconomic status influencing health outcomes through access to healthcare. This is an example of an indirect association because the relationship between socioeconomic status and health outcomes is mediated by a third variable, such as access to healthcare. The association is real, but it is not direct.

Alcohol consumption directly causing liver cirrhosis: This is an example of a direct association, where alcohol directly damages liver cells, leading to cirrhosis. There is no intermediary variable mediating this relationship.

Asbestos exposure consistently associated with mesothelioma: This is a direct association. Asbestos fibres cause cellular damage in the pleura, leading to mesothelioma without the involvement of a third variable.

Increased ice cream sales correlating with drowning deaths due to warm weather: This is an example of a spurious association. The observed relationship is caused by a confounding variable (warm weather), not a true link between ice cream sales and drowning deaths.

Smoking directly causing lung cancer: This is a direct association. Carcinogens in cigarette smoke directly damage lung tissue and DNA, leading to cancer without any intermediary factor.
