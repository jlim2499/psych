Which of the following is true of population parameters?

The mean height of a population is an example of a parameter

The correct answer is The mean height of a population is an example of a parameter. A parameter is a numerical characteristic or value that describes an entire population. Examples include the population mean, variance, or proportion. The mean height of a population is a classic example of a parameter, as it describes a feature of the whole population rather than a sample.

Parameters tend to have normal distributions is incorrect. Parameters themselves are fixed values that describe a population and do not have distributions. However, estimates of parameters derived from samples (statistics) may follow certain distributions, such as a normal distribution under the Central Limit Theorem.

Parameters are the result of statistical tests is incorrect. Parameters are inherent characteristics of the population and not derived from statistical tests. Statistical tests are used to estimate or make inferences about population parameters based on sample data.

Parameters are always whole numbers is incorrect. Parameters can take on any value, whether whole numbers or decimals, depending on the variable being measured. For example, a population mean or proportion can often be a fractional value.

A parameter is a statistic is incorrect. A parameter and a statistic are distinct. A parameter describes a population, whereas a statistic is a calculated value from a sample that is used to estimate a population parameter.
