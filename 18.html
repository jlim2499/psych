Which of the following suggests a pseudohallucination rather than a true one?

Occurs in inner subjective space

The correct answer is Occurs in inner subjective space. Pseudohallucinations are a type of hallucination that the person experiencing them recognises as not being real. They typically occur in the 'inner subjective space', which means they are perceived internally, rather than externally in the environment around the person. This differentiates pseudohallucinations from true hallucinations, where the individual believes what they are perceiving is real and it appears to be located in external objective space.

Is concrete is incorrect because both true hallucinations and pseudohallucinations can be concrete or abstract. The term 'concrete' refers to something that can be perceived by the senses, such as an object or sound, whereas 'abstract' refers to something that cannot be perceived by the senses, such as a concept or idea. Whether a hallucination is concrete or abstract does not differentiate between a true hallucination and a pseudohallucination.

Is vivid is also incorrect because both true hallucinations and pseudohallucinations can be vivid. Vividness refers to how clear and detailed a perception is, whether it's a real perception or a hallucination. A vivid hallucination might seem more 'real', but this doesn't necessarily mean it's a true hallucination rather than a pseudohallucination.

The ability to change or control a hallucination at will varies widely among individuals and situations, regardless of whether they're experiencing true or pseudohallucinations.
