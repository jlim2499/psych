In pregnancy, women undergo screening to assess whether their fetus is likely to have Down's Syndrome. The screening test evaluates levels of specific hormones in the blood. Screening test results are reported as positive or negative, indicating that a women is more or less likely to be carrying an affected fetus.

A study is done to evaluate the validity of the test. A population of 4,810 pregnant women undergo the screening test and are scored as either positive or negative depending on the levels of hormones in the blood. In addition, each woman is followed to birth to determine whether the fetus was, in fact, affected with Down's Syndrome.

The study reports that 10 babies were found on examination to have Down's Syndrome. The screening test produced a positive result in 9 of these. Of those deemed to have been unaffected the screening test produced a positive result in 351 cases.

Based on this information calculate the following.
