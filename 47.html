A study reports a mean score of 30 with a standard deviation of 6 in a sample of 36 patients. What is the standard error of the mean (SEM)?

1

The correct answer is 1. The standard error of the mean (SEM) is calculated using the formula:

SEM = Standard Deviation (SD) / √Sample Size (n)

Given:

Standard deviation (SD) = 6
Sample size (n) = 36

SEM = 6 / √36 = 6 / 6 = 1

0.5: This underestimates the SEM. It would require either a larger sample size or a smaller standard deviation to produce this result.

1.5: This overestimates the SEM. It would imply either a smaller sample size or a larger standard deviation than provided.

6: This is the standard deviation, not the SEM. SEM accounts for the size of the sample, making it smaller than the standard deviation in this case.

36: This is the sample size, not the SEM. SEM is derived by dividing the standard deviation by the square root of the sample size.
