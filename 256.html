A study is done to compare the use of bedside urine dipstick drug screens to detect cannabis use against the gold standard of urine laboratory confirmatory testing. Over a two month period, 1200 community psychiatric patients are tested by both methods simultaneously. The gold standard method detects cannabis in just 300 samples whereas the bedside test produces a positive result in 720 samples (600 of these are considered to be false positives).

Based on this information calculate the following

312.The pre-test probability of cannabis use in the sample under study
25% Where additional clinical data are lacking, the pre-test probability is approximated by the prevalence.

The gold standard test revealed 300 cases of cannabis use in 1200 patients which is 25%.

314.Likelihood ratio for a positive test result

To calculate the likelihood ratio for a positive test result (LR+):

LR+ = true positive rate / false positive rate

LR+ = sensitivity / (1 - specificity)

Sensitivity = 120 / 300 = 0.4

Specificity = 300 / 900 = 1/3 = 0.33

LR+ = 0.4 / (1 - 0.33) = 0.4 / 0.67 = 0.6

Therefore, the likelihood ratio for a positive test result is approximately 0.60.
