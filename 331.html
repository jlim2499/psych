A study looks at the chance of having a stroke in elderly patients prescribed antipsychotic medication. Group A are given standard treatment. After 5 years 20 of the 100 patients have had a stroke. Group B have standard treatment plus a new drug designed to reduce the risk of a stroke. After 5 years 10 of the 60 patients have had an stroke.
What are the odds of having a stroke whilst taking the new drug compared to the odds of having a stroke in those receiving treatment as usual?




0.8

Odds - remember a ratio of the number of people who incur a particular outcome to the number of people who do not incur the outcome

NOT a ratio of the number of people who incur a particular outcome to the total number of people
Odds of stroke in group B (new drug) = 10/50 = 1/5

Odds of stroke in group A (treatment as usual) = 20/80 = 1/4

Odds ratio of having a stroke = 1/5 divided by 1/4 = 0.8

An odds ratio of < 1 implies that the risk of stroke is less in those exposed to the new drug compared to those receiving standard treatment.
