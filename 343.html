A masters student had noticed that nearly all of her patients with panic disorder were married. She was keen to investigate this further to see if there was an association.
She selected 100 patients with panic disorder and 100 controls. Of the 100 patients with panic disorder, 85 were married. Of the 100 controls, only 60 were married.

What is the odds ratio?


3.77

The correct answer is 3.77. The odds ratio (OR) is calculated by comparing the odds of being married among those with panic disorder to the odds of being married among the control group.

To calculate the odds ratio:

For the panic disorder group: 85 married out of 100, so the odds of being married is 85/15 = 5.67.

For the control group: 60 married out of 100, so the odds of being married is 60/40 = 1.5.

The odds ratio is then calculated as 5.67 / 1.5 = 3.77.

This indicates that patients with panic disorder have 3.77 times higher odds of being married compared to the control group.
