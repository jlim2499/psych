A researcher conducts a study looking into the age of diagnosis of adult ADHD. The results are normally distributed with a mean of 40 and a standard deviation of 5. In this distribution, 68% of the distribution would be found between what two numbers?

35 and 45

The correct answer is 35 and 45. In a normal distribution, 68% of the data lies within one standard deviation (σ) of the mean (μ). Given a mean of 40 and a standard deviation of 5, the range can be calculated as follows:

Lower bound = μ - σ = 40 - 5 = 35
Upper bound = μ + σ = 40 + 5 = 45

Therefore, 68% of the distribution would be found between 35 and 45.
