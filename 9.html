In an effort to try and estimate the average height of patients with schizophrenia, a medical student conducts a small study within a psychiatric hospital. The hospital has a total of 245 patients. The student creates a list of all the patients and assigns them all a unique identification number. Patients are then selected at random to produce a sample of 20. The height of each patient is then recorded and the mean score calculated.
How is the sampling strategy of the study best described?


Simple random sampling


Explanation for the Correct Answer:

Simple random sampling: The study described involves assigning each patient a unique identification number and then selecting patients at random to produce a sample. This process indicates that each patient has an equal chance of being selected, which is the defining characteristic of simple random sampling. Hence, Option 3 is the correct answer.

Explanation for the Incorrect Answers:

Stratified sampling: This would involve dividing the population into subgroups based on certain characteristics, and then randomly sampling from each subgroup. The description does not indicate that any stratification was used.

Multilevel sampling: Multilevel sampling involves sampling within several hierarchical levels, such as regions, clusters within regions, and then subjects within clusters. This is not indicated in the study description.

Cluster sampling: In cluster sampling, the population is divided into clusters, and a random sample of clusters is chosen, with all members of selected clusters being included. This method was not described.

Systematic sampling: This method involves selecting every nth individual from a list. The description does not specify that every nth patient was selected, but rather that patients were chosen at random, which does not suggest a systematic approach.
