A junior psychiatrist is keen to explore the attitudes of patients' towards mobile healthcare applications. They post a leaflet in a general outpatient clinic waiting area with instructions on how to fill out the on-line survey.
After three months they evaluate the survey results and find that more than 80% of those who responded said they would favour the introduction of more mobile healthcare applications. They conclude that this result should lead to the development of more of mobile technologies for their patient population.

Which of the following types of bias would have the greatest potential to affect the results of this study?


Selection bias

The aim of the study is to get a broad estimate of the patients' attitudes. To get an accurate estimate we need a sample that broadly reflects the population from which it was drawn. One may assume that this study method may have resulted in a skewed sample as certain patients (such as those who are not computer literate) may not have been included in the sample.
