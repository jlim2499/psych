Which of the following is an advantage of performing a cost-effectiveness analysis?

Outcomes are expressed in natural units that are clinically meaningful

The main advantage of a cost-effectiveness analysis is that the outcomes are immediately meaningful e.g. pounds spent per year of remission from depression.

With economic evaluations, the estimate of costs is fairly standardised across the different types of studies. Various costs need to be factored in such as 'direct costs' (e.g. doctor's time), 'indirect costs' (e.g. productivity issues due to being off work), and 'future costs' (e.g. development of diabetes as a consequence of treatment with clozapine).

The different economic evaluations vary mainly due to the way in which outcomes are assessed.
