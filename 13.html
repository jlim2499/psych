A study published in 2004 compared the use of the CAGE questionnaire against a tool called the MINI which was considered the gold standard. Based on the table (below) answer the questions that follow.



13.Using a CAGE cut-off score of ≥ (greater than or equal to)1, what is the sensitivity of the CAGE tool for detecting alcohol dependence in this study?
94%

If you get this correct then you can officially say you understand sensitivity etc (and that you're pretty dam smart).

The trick for answering these questions is to understand that if say a cut off score of 3 is used then this will include all those who answered 3 and those who answer 4. You then need to add these figures together to create a 2x2 table (as you always do with these questions).

Here is the actual table from the study (if you're interested). For extra practice why not try and complete the entire table without looking.


Sensitivity = TP/(TP+FN)

If a cut off of 1 is used then this will identify all those who would have been picked up with a score of 1, 2, 3, and 4.

The true positive rate is therefore 45 (as a cut off of 1 would only miss 3).

The total of those who had alcohol dependence was 48.

So sensitivity = 45/48
= 0.9375 or 94%


14.Using a CAGE cut-off score of ≥ (greater than or equal to) 3, what is the specificity of the CAGE tool for detecting alcohol dependence in this study?
97% Specificity = TN/(FP+TN)

The total of absent cases = 582 + 44 + 34 + 18 + 3 = 681

If cut off of 3 is used then the true negative = 681 - (18 + 3) = 660

So specificity = 660/681
= 0.97 or 97%




15.For a CAGE score of ≥ (greater than or equal to) 2, what is the positive predictive value of the tool?
42% PPV = TP/(TP+FP)

TP = 14 + 20 + 6 = 40
FP = 34 + 18 + 3 = 55

so PPV = 40 / (40 + 55)
= 0.42 or 42%
