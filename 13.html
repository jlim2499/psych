Regarding IQ testing, if a person scores 100, what percentage of people (on average) would be expected to score below this mark?

50%

The correct answer is 50%. The IQ score is based on a standardised scale with a mean of 100 and a standard deviation of 15. Therefore, an individual who scores exactly 100 is at the exact middle of the distribution, meaning that approximately half (50%) of the population would score below this mark.
