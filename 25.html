In a normal distribution what percentage of values lie within 3 standard deviations of the mean?

99.7%

The correct answer is 99.7%. In a normal distribution, approximately 68% of values lie within one standard deviation of the mean, about 95% lie within two standard deviations, and around 99.7% fall within three standard deviations. This is often referred to as the empirical rule or the 68-95-99.7 rule in statistics.

The option 68.3% would be correct if the question asked for the percentage of values that lie within one standard deviation from the mean, not three. In a normal distribution, this represents the data that falls between -1 and +1 standard deviations from the mean.

Option 98.3% does not correspond to any specific range within a standard normal distribution curve and therefore is incorrect.

However, it's not accurate for three standard deviations which was asked in this question.
