A new drug is trialled for the treatment of lung cancer. Drug A is given to 500 people with early-stage non-small cell lung cancer, and a placebo is given to 450 people with the same condition. After 5 years, 300 people who received Drug A were still alive, compared to 225 who received the placebo.
What is the number needed to treat (NNT) to achieve one additional survivor after 5 years?


10

NNT = 1 / ARR
The correct answer is 10. The Number Needed to Treat (NNT) is calculated as the reciprocal of the absolute risk reduction (ARR). In this case, the survival rate with Drug A is 300 out of 500 = 60%, and the survival rate with placebo is 225 out of 450 = 50%. The absolute risk reduction is:

ARR = 60% − 50% = 10% (or 0.10)
NNT = 1 ÷ ARR = 1 ÷ 0.10 = 10

This means that for every 10 people treated with Drug A, one additional person survives compared to those treated with placebo. This metric is critical in clinical decision-making, allowing psychiatrists and other clinicians to weigh the benefit of interventions in real-world terms and to prioritise treatments that offer meaningful outcome improvements.
