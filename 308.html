A study on the effectiveness of a new psychotherapy technique fails to show a significant difference compared to standard therapy. To increase the power of a subsequent study, the researchers should:

Increase the sample size

Previous Exam Question

The correct answer is Increase the sample size. Increasing the sample size reduces the variability of parameter estimates, making it easier to detect a true effect and increasing the power of the study. A larger sample size decreases the likelihood of a Type II error (false negative), ensuring that a real difference is more likely to be identified if it exists.

Decrease the effect size: Decreasing the effect size does not increase power. In fact, detecting smaller effect sizes requires greater power, which is often achieved by increasing the sample size. Effect size is a characteristic of the study and is not something researchers can manipulate after the fact.

Lower the significance level from 0.05 to 0.01: Lowering the significance level reduces the likelihood of a Type I error (false positive) but also decreases power, as it makes the criteria for rejecting the null hypothesis stricter. This would make it harder, not easier, to detect a true effect.

Increase the variability of the sample: Increasing variability decreases power, as it increases the noise in the data, making it harder to detect a true effect. Reducing variability through careful sample selection and measurement is generally more beneficial for increasing power.

Use a less sensitive outcome measure: Using a less sensitive outcome measure reduces the ability to detect differences between groups, thereby lowering power. Researchers should aim to use more sensitive and precise measures to improve power.
