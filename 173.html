Which of the following is true of a type II error?

Occurs when the null hypothesis is incorrectly accepted

Hypothesis testing can lead to two types of error, known as type I and type II errors.

A type I error is made when one incorrectly rejects the null hypothesis. Or incorrectly accepts the alternative hypothesis. It is also known as an alpha error, error of the first kind, or a false positive.

A type II error is made when one incorrectly accepts the null hypothesis. It is also known as the beta error, error of the second kind or the false negative
