An intervention improves a patient's QoL from 0.1 to 0.3 over a period of 5 years. What is the incremental QALY gained due to the intervention?

1 QALY

The correct answer is 1 QALY. The incremental QALY (Quality-Adjusted Life Year) gained due to the intervention is calculated by multiplying the improvement in quality of life (0.3 - 0.1 = 0.2) by the duration of the intervention (5 years). Thus, 0.2 Ã— 5 = 1 QALY. This metric is critical in health economics to assess the cost-effectiveness of interventions by quantifying the quality and quantity of life added.
