An indirect association occurs when:

Two variables are related, but the relationship is mediated by a third variable

The correct answer is Two variables are related, but the relationship is mediated by a third variable. An indirect association occurs when two variables are genuinely related, but their relationship is explained or mediated by a third variable. For example, socioeconomic status influences health outcomes indirectly through mediating factors like access to healthcare or lifestyle choices.

There is no true relationship between two variables: This describes a spurious association, where the observed relationship is false or misleading, often due to a confounder or random chance.

The relationship is due to random chance: Random chance can produce spurious associations but does not define an indirect association.

One variable directly causes changes in another: This describes a direct association, where the relationship is causal and unmediated by a third variable.

The association is observed across multiple studies: Observing an association across studies is related to consistency, a Bradford Hill criterion, and does not define whether the association is direct, indirect, or spurious.
