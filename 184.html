Which of the following is a quality measure for the reporting of randomised control trials?

CONSORT

Explanation for the Correct Answer:

CONSORT (Consolidated Standards of Reporting Trials) is the correct option. It provides a set of guidelines designed to improve the transparency and completeness of the reporting of randomised controlled trials. The CONSORT Statement comprises a 25-item checklist and a flow diagram, which authors are recommended to include in their reports.

Explanation for the Incorrect Answers:

STROBE (Strengthening the Reporting of Observational Studies in Epidemiology) applies to observational studies, not randomised controlled trials.

SQUIRE (Standards for QUality Improvement Reporting Excellence) is for reporting on systematic efforts to improve the quality and safety of health care services, not randomised trials.

MOOSE (Meta-analysis Of Observational Studies in Epidemiology) is for the reporting of meta-analyses of observational studies, not randomised control trials.

PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) is intended for systematic reviews and meta-analyses, not for reporting on individual randomised controlled trials.
