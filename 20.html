You asses a 92 year old man at the request of his medical team as they are concerned about his presentation. Following an introduction you ask the man why he is in hospital. He gives a very long winded answer and includes lots of unnecessary detail before finally telling you he had a chest infection. He continues in the same way throughout the remainder of the interview, frequently going off topic but always eventually coming back to the original point.
Which of the following does he exhibit?


Circumstantiality

The correct answer is Circumstantiality. Circumstantiality refers to a style of speech characterized by indirectness and excessive detail. Individuals with circumstantial speech can be excessively detailed and lengthy, but they eventually return to the point. This is exactly what the patient in the scenario is exhibiting: he provides unnecessary details and takes a long time to arrive at his point, but he does eventually get there.

Flight of ideas is incorrect because it refers to a rapid shift in thoughts with only superficial associative connections between them that are often perceived by others as incoherent or confusing. While the patient's speech may seem confusing due to its lengthiness and excessive detail, it does not exhibit the rapid shifting from one unrelated topic to another that characterises flight of ideas.

Linear speech is also incorrect as this term describes a coherent, logical and sequential pattern of thought that is clearly communicated. The patient's speech, while ultimately reaching a conclusion, is not direct or succinct - it involves much unnecessary detail before arriving at the point.

Perseveration would be an incorrect choice because perseveration refers to the pathological, persistent repetition of a word, phrase, or gesture despite cessation or absence of the original stimulus. For instance, patients might keep going back to the same topic even when conversation has moved on. In this scenario, although our patient gives long answers filled with unnecessary details, he doesn't repetitively stick on one particular word or phrase.

Finally, Tangentiality is also incorrect because tangential speech refers to communication that wanders off topic and never returns to it or provides irrelevant responses which do not address original question properly. It differs from circumstantiality in that tangential speech never comes back around to address the initial topic while circumstantial speech does.
