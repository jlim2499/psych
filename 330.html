A researcher conducts a study looking into the number of cigarettes smoked each day by inpatients with a diagnosis of schizophrenia. The results are normally distributed with a mean of 20 and a standard deviation of 3. Approximately 95% of the distribution would be found between what two numbers?

14 and 26

The correct answer is 14 and 26. Approximately 95% of the distribution lies within two standard deviations of the mean in a normal distribution. Given the mean (μ) is 20 and the standard deviation (σ) is 3, the range can be calculated as follows:

Lower bound = μ - 2σ = 20 - 2(3) = 20 - 6 = 14
Upper bound = μ + 2σ = 20 + 2(3) = 20 + 6 = 26

Therefore, 95% of the distribution would be found between 14 and 26.
