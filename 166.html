Which of the following is true regarding measures of effect?

Relative risk can be used to measure effect in randomised control trials

Relative risk can be used to measure effect in cohort, cross-sectional and randomised control trials (but not case-control studies).

Neither the risk ratio nor the odds ratio can be calculated for a study if there are no events in the control group.

The odds ratio is always more extreme than the relative risk (i.e. away from null value of 1). They always tend to overestimate effects.
