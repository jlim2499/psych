A senior psychiatrist, is explaining a 95% confidence interval (CI) to a junior doctor who brought a research article about a new ADHD medication. The study shows an average symptom reduction with a 95% CI of [15, 25]. The junior asks if this means there's a 95% chance that their patient’s symptoms will reduce within that range when taking the drug.
Which of the following best explains the meaning of the 95% CI in this context?


If the study was repeated an infinite number of times, 95% of the confidence intervals calculated would contain the true average symptom reduction

The correct answer is If the study was repeated an infinite number of times, 95% of the confidence intervals calculated would contain the true average symptom reduction. This option correctly describes the concept of a 95% confidence interval (CI). It means that if we could hypothetically repeat the study many times, 95% of those calculated CIs would include the true average effect of the treatment for the entire population. This interpretation is crucial in understanding CIs as measures of the precision of an estimate for a population parameter, not as predictive tools for individual patient outcomes. Clinically, this reinforces the importance of understanding that CIs reflect group-level data and help us generalise findings to a larger population, rather than predicting individual patient responses.
