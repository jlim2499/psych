A new test to screen for eating disorders in patients with a positive family history is tested on 920 patients. The test is positive in 16 of the 20 patients who are proven to have an eating disorder. Of the remaining patients, only 10 have a positive test. What is the negative predictive value of the new test?

890/894 = 99.6%

The correct answer is 890/894 = 99.6%. This is the correct calculation for the negative predictive value (NPV), which is the proportion of patients who tested negative and indeed do not have the disorder. The formula for NPV is True Negatives / (False Negatives + True Negatives), which in this case is 890 / (4 + 890) = 99.6%.
