If a disease has a standard mortality ratio (SMR) of 1.4 in a sampled population, which of the following statements is true?

There were 40% more fatalities from the disease in this population compared to the reference population

The correct answer is There were 40% more fatalities from the disease in this population compared to the reference population. An SMR of 1.4 indicates that the observed mortality in the study population is 40% higher than what would be expected based on the mortality rates of a reference population.

The disease caused 140 deaths in the sample population during the study period is incorrect. The SMR does not provide the absolute number of deaths; it provides a ratio of observed to expected deaths.

Mortality from the disease in the sampled population is lower than would be expected is incorrect. An SMR of 1.4 indicates higher, not lower, mortality than expected.

Mortality from the disease in the sampled population is as would be expected is incorrect. An SMR of 1.4 means that mortality is higher than expected, not the same.

There were 140% more fatalities from the disease in this population compared to the reference population is incorrect. An SMR of 1.4 means there were 40% more fatalities, not 140%.
