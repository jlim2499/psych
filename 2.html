A case control study looks at the use of amoxicillin in the treatment of acute sinusitis compared to placebo. The following results are obtained:
Total number of patients Number who achieved resolution of symptoms at 7 days
Amoxicillin 10060
Placebo7530

What are the odds of a patient achieving resolution of symptoms at 7 days if they take amoxicillin compared to the odds of achieving remission in those taking placebo?


2.25

Odds - remember a ratio of the number of people who incur a particular outcome to the number of people who do not incur the outcome

NOT a ratio of the number of people who incur a particular outcome to the total number of people
To assist you in a question like this the safest approach is to draw the 2 x 2 table and fill in the spaces, then use the formula.


Outcome yes (symptom resolution) Outcome no (no resolution)
Exposure yes (amoxicillin) 6040
Exposure no (placebo)3045

Odds ratio (OR) = (a/b) / (c/d)

= (60/40) / (30/45)

= 2.25

Interpretation: Individuals with symptom resolution were 2.25 times more likely to have been exposed to amoxicillin.

Note: It would not be appropriate to say individuals exposed to amoxicillin were 2.25 times more likely to achieve symptom resolution because an odds ratio is calculated from a case control study which begins from the outcome.
The odds of symptoms resolution with amoxicillin vs placebo = 60 / 30

The odds of symptoms resolution with placebo = 30 / 45



Therefore the odds ratio = 2.25
