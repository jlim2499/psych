Which of the following is calculated by dividing the standard deviation by the square root of the sample size?

Standard error

Standard error of the mean = standard deviation / square root (number of patients)
The correct answer is Standard error. Standard error is a statistical term that measures the accuracy with which a sample represents a population. In statistics, standard error is typically computed by dividing the standard deviation by the square root of the sample size (n). It provides an estimate of the variability likely to be encountered from one random sample to another and serves as a measure of uncertainty.

The first incorrect option is Mean. The mean, often referred to as the average, is calculated by adding up all numbers in a data set and then dividing by the count of those numbers. It gives us a central value of a data set but does not involve standard deviation or square root of sample size in its calculation.

Next, we have Mode, which also does not fit our equation. The mode in statistics refers to the number or value that appears most frequently in a data set. A set may have one mode, more than one mode, or no mode at all. It does not involve any division or square roots and thus it cannot be the correct answer.

Range is another incorrect choice. The range in statistics refers to the difference between the highest and lowest values in a dataset. Like mean and mode, it doesn't involve standard deviation or square root operations.

Finally, there's Variance. Variance measures how spread out the members of a data set are from the mean. However, variance involves squaring each difference between individual observations and their mean rather than taking square root or performing division operations involved in calculating standard error.

In summary, each term - mean, mode, range and variance - has distinct calculations that do not match with our given equation for standard error.
