The Clifton Assessment Procedure is an assessment tool used in which of the following settings?

Nursing homes for the elderly

The correct answer is Nursing homes for the elderly. The Clifton Assessment Procedure for the Elderly (CAPE) is specifically designed to assess the quality of life, cognitive deficits, and physical dependency levels in elderly patients. It is particularly useful in nursing homes where it can help in planning care and identifying areas of unmet need, ensuring that elderly residents receive appropriate support and interventions.

On hospital wards for people with a high risk of self harm is not correct. While assessment tools are crucial in these settings, the CAPE is not designed for evaluating risk of self harm but rather for assessing cognitive and physical needs in the elderly.

Schizophrenia rehabilitation units is incorrect. The CAPE is not intended for use in schizophrenia rehabilitation. These units require specialised tools that focus on the specific needs of patients with schizophrenia, such as evaluating the effectiveness of antipsychotic treatment and managing symptoms.

Prisons for substance abusers is not accurate. The CAPE does not address the unique needs of substance abusers in prison settings. Instead, other assessment tools designed to evaluate addiction severity and readiness for treatment would be more appropriate.

Residential homes for people with learning disabilities is also incorrect. The CAPE focuses on the elderly population, particularly those in nursing homes, rather than individuals with learning disabilities. Tools tailored to assess learning disabilities are more suitable for these residential settings.
