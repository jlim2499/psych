Regarding the interquartile range, what proportion of a data set have a value below the second quartile?

50%

The correct answer is 50%. The interquartile range is a statistical measure that represents the middle 50% of a data set. It is calculated by subtracting the first quartile (25th percentile) from the third quartile (75th percentile). Therefore, by definition, 50% of values lie below the second quartile (which is also known as the median), and 50% of values lie above it.

The option 25% incorrectly suggests that only a quarter of the data lies below the second quartile. This would be true for the first quartile, not the second.

The option 75% overstates the proportion of data lying below the second quartile. While it's true that 75% of data lies below the third quartile, this does not apply to the second quartile or median.

Similarly, both options 10% and 60% are incorrect as they do not align with any specific percentiles in relation to quartiles within a dataset. The only fixed percentage points within a dataset are at each of its quartiles: 25%, 50%, and 75%.
