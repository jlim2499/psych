In an attempt to better understand the religious needs of her patients, a psychiatrist creates a Likert scale questionnaire. The questionnaire is then given to 100 community patients. How is the data collected by the questionnaire best described?

Ordinal

Likert scales are ordinal scales. A Likert scale offers respondents a number of options to each question. For example:

Question:

How often do you pray?

Answer:

Never
Rarely
Occasionally
Frequently
Daily

Such data is ordered (daily is more often than occasionally) but there is no consistent quantifiable difference between each option (the difference between daily and frequently is not necessarily the same as that between Rarely and occasionally).
