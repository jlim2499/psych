A new blood test to screen patients for early Alzheimer's dementia is trialled on 500 patients. The test was positive in 40 of the 50 patients shown to have early Alzheimer's by standard clinical assessment. It was also positive in 20 patients who were shown not to have early Alzheimer's. What is the positive predictive value of the test?



0.66

The correct answer is 0.66. Positive predictive value (PPV) is the probability that subjects with a positive screening test truly have the disease. It is calculated as the number of true positives (those who have the disease and test positive) divided by the total number of positive tests results, both true and false. In this scenario, there were 40 true positives (patients with early Alzheimer's who tested positive) and 20 false positives (patients without Alzheimer's who tested positive), giving a PPV of 40 / (40 + 20) = 0.66.

Sensitivity is the ability of a test to correctly identify those with the disease, calculated as true positives / (true positives + false negatives). Here, there are no false negatives mentioned so it cannot be calculated.
