Which of the following tests primarily assesses executive function?

Tower of London test

The correct answer is the Tower of London test. This test was specifically designed to evaluate executive functions, particularly planning and problem-solving skills. The Tower of London test requires the participant to move discs on three pegs to match a given pattern, using as few moves as possible. It assesses the ability to plan ahead and strategise, which are key aspects of executive function.

The first incorrect option is the Continuous Performance Task. While this test does involve elements of attention and impulsivity control, which are components of executive function, it primarily measures sustained and selective attention rather than executive function as a whole. Participants are required to respond rapidly to specific stimuli while ignoring others over an extended period, making it more of an attentional task.

The second incorrect option is the Halstead-Reitan battery. This is a neuropsychological test battery used to evaluate a range of cognitive functions including attention, memory, spatial processing, and motor skills but not primarily focused on executive function.

The third incorrect option is Raven's Progressive Matrices. This test primarily measures abstract reasoning abilities and non-verbal intelligence. It involves identifying patterns within matrices and selecting the missing piece from several options. While it may tap into some aspects of executive functioning such as problem-solving, its primary focus is not on executive function.

Lastly, we have the Rey-Osterrieth Complex Figure test, which primarily assesses visuospatial constructional ability and visual memory. In this task, individuals are asked to copy a complex figure (testing visuoconstructional ability) and then draw it from memory (testing visual memory). Although these tasks do require some degree of planning (an aspect of executive function), they do not fully or directly measure overall executive functioning like the Tower of London Test does.
