Suggesting publication bias based solely on the forest plot is speculative and risks undermining the perceived reliability of the findings without proper statistical evaluation.

Furthermore, while Su 2003 contributes significantly to the pooled result with a high effect size, its square box is not visible on the forest plot. This is because its effect size (2.067) and upper confidence limit (3.105) fall beyond the axis limit of +2.00, which is the maximum shown on the x-axis. In such cases, forest plots typically represent these studies with a horizontal line ending in an arrow, indicating that the point estimate and confidence interval extend beyond the displayed range. This is a standard visual convention and does not imply exclusion from the analysis. Understanding this nuance prevents clinicians from mistakenly assuming a study has been omitted when it simply lies beyond the scale.
