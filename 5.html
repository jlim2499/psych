A null hypothesis can only be rejected at the 5% significance level if?

A 95% confidence interval does not include the hypothesised value of the parameter

The correct answer is A 95% confidence interval does not include the hypothesised value of the parameter. This is because in statistical hypothesis testing, a null hypothesis is rejected if the test statistic falls in the critical region. The critical region corresponds to the rejection of the null hypothesis at a certain significance level. If we are working with a 95% confidence interval and our hypothesised value does not lie within this interval, we can reject the null hypothesis at the 5% significance level. This means that there's less than a 5% chance that our observed data could have occurred under the null hypothesis.
