A study is performed looking at the validity of a new diagnostic test for epilepsy. The study designers are concerned that not all patients may receive the gold-standard existing test. What type of bias does this describe?

Work-up bias

Explanation for the Correct Answer:

Work-up bias: This occurs when the selection of patients for a study is influenced by the results of a preliminary test. In the scenario described, the concern is that not all patients may receive the existing gold-standard test, which may lead to an overestimation or underestimation of the new test's validity. This form of bias is particularly relevant when a diagnostic test is being validated because the inclusion of patients can be skewed based on whether they received the gold-standard test.

Explanation for the Incorrect Answers:

Attention bias: This type of bias occurs when participants in a study are more likely to notice and report certain outcomes, which is not the concern described in the scenario.

Selection bias: This occurs when the participants selected for a study are not representative of the general population. While this can affect study validity, it is not specific to the issue of patients not receiving the gold-standard test as in the scenario.

Co-intervention bias: This happens when patients receive additional interventions alongside the intervention being studied, which can confound the results. It is not relevant to the scenario described.

Instrument bias: This refers to a systematic error caused by flaws in the measurement tool itself, not by the selection of patients for different tests.
