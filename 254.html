A study was conducted to explore the association between body mass index (BMI) and mortality in patients with schizophrenia. The study began with a group of 1000 patients with schizophrenia. These patients were all assessed by taking measurements of weight and height and the BMI then calculated. Subsequently, subjects were followed-up until a maximum of 15 years after the start of the study. BMI levels were divided into 3 categories (high, average, low). It was shown that, adjusted for age, sex, treatment modality and co-morbidity, a high BMI at baseline was associated with a 2-fold increase in mortality.
How is this study best described?


Prospective cohort study

The study is observational and not experimental as the researchers simply observe and do not manipulate any risk factors or interventions.

The study goes forward in time and so is prospective.

The study begins with a group (cohort) and follows them up to explore the effect of exposure. In this case the exposure the BMI.
