A study is performed looking at the chance of stroke in high-risk psychiatric patients taking a new oral antithrombotic drug compared to warfarin. The following results are obtained:

What is the relative risk of having a stroke within a 3 year period for patients taking the new drug compared to warfarin?


2.5

RR = EER / CER
The correct answer is 2.5.

To calculate the relative risk (RR), we compare the risk of having a stroke within a 3-year period for patients taking the new drug versus those taking warfarin. The formula for RR is:
RR = (Risk in the treatment group) / (Risk in the control group)

Here, the risk in the treatment group (new drug) is:
Risk (new drug) = 10 / (10 + 190) = 10 / 200 = 0.05

The risk in the control group (warfarin) is:
Risk (warfarin) = 12 / (12 + 588) = 12 / 600 = 0.02

Now, calculate RR:
RR = 0.05 / 0.02 = 2.5

This means the risk of having a stroke is 2.5 times higher for patients taking the new drug compared to those taking warfarin.
