A range of 1 standard deviation above and below the mean includes what percentage of the values?

68.2 %

The correct answer is 68.2 %. In a normal distribution, 1 standard deviation above and below the mean includes approximately 68.2% of the values. This is a fundamental concept in statistics and is essential for understanding data variability, confidence intervals, and hypothesis testing in medical research.

The option 72.6 % is incorrect. There isn't a standard statistical rule or principle that associates this particular percentage with the range of 1 standard deviation from the mean in a normal distribution.

The option 95.4 % is also incorrect but it's close to an important value in statistics. Approximately 95% (more precisely 95.45%) of values lie within 2 standard deviations from the mean in a normal distribution, not one.

Similarly, 84.2 % doesn't correspond to any known statistical rule related to standard deviations in a normal distribution, making it an incorrect choice as well.

Lastly, 99.7 %, although incorrect for this question, represents another key principle - The empirical rule or three-sigma rule states that nearly all values (99.7%) lie within three standard deviations from the mean in a normally distributed set of data.

Understanding these percentages and their relation to standard deviations from the mean are crucial for interpreting many types of medical research findings and for conducting your own investigations.
