In a study of children with anxiety disorders, 76% of those receiving fluvoxamine and 29% of those receiving placebo were treatment responders. What is the number needed to treat (NNT)?

3

The correct answer is 3.

The Number Needed to Treat (NNT) is calculated from the Absolute Risk Reduction (ARR), which is the difference in treatment response rates.

Fluoxetine response rate = 76%

Placebo response rate = 29%

ARR = 76% – 29% = 47% = 0.47

NNT = 1 / ARR = 1 / 0.47 ≈ 2.13

By convention, NNT is always rounded up, even if the result is just slightly over a whole number.

So, NNT = 3

This means that for every 3 children treated with fluvoxamine, 1 additional child will respond compared to placebo.

In contrast, for Number Needed to Harm (NNH), the convention is to round down, which avoids underestimating potential harm.
