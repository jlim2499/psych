Which of the following describes a spurious association?

A relationship that appears to exist but is caused by a confounder or random chance

The correct answer is A relationship that appears to exist but is caused by a confounder or random chance. A spurious association occurs when two variables seem to be related, but this relationship is false or misleading, often due to confounding factors or random chance. For example, the correlation between ice cream sales and drowning deaths is spurious because both are influenced by hot weather, not by a direct or indirect relationship between the two variables.
