Which of the following is true regarding correlation and regression?

The correlation type is independent of the strength

The correct answer is: The correlation type is independent of the strength. The type of correlation, such as Pearson's, Spearman's, or Kendall's, is a separate consideration from how strong the correlation is (the correlation coefficient). Each type of correlation measures the association between variables in different ways, suitable for the data's characteristics.

A correlation coefficient of 0.2 does not imply a strong correlation. In most contexts, a correlation coefficient of 0.2 is considered a weak association between two variables.

When constructing a scatter graph, it is conventional to put the independent variable on the horizontal axis (X-axis) and the dependent variable on the vertical axis (Y-axis), not the other way around.

The correlation coefficient is not an appropriate summary of association if a scatter plot has a non-linear pattern. The correlation coefficient assumes a linear relationship between variables; for non-linear relationships, other statistical measures are needed to summarise the association.

The correlation coefficient is always between +1 and -1, not +10 and -10. The value of +1 indicates a perfect positive linear relationship, -1 indicates a perfect negative linear relationship, and 0 indicates no linear relationship.
