In the context of the Nolan Model for Improvement, which of the following statements is true regarding the 'Three Fundamental Questions'?

They emphasise defining clear aims, establishing measurement criteria, and identifying actionable changes

The correct answer is They emphasise defining clear aims, establishing measurement criteria, and identifying actionable changes. Nolan’s Model for Improvement revolves around the 'Three Fundamental Questions': defining what you are trying to accomplish (clear aims), determining how you will measure improvement (measurement criteria), and identifying the changes that can lead to improvement (actionable changes). These questions provide a structured framework for driving effective and sustainable improvements.

They prioritise selecting changes based on convenience rather than evidence: This is incorrect. The Nolan Model encourages evidence-based decision-making and utilises data to guide the selection and evaluation of changes rather than relying on convenience or untested ideas.

They are optional guidelines that can be bypassed if the team has prior improvement experience: This is incorrect. The 'Three Fundamental Questions' form the core of the improvement framework and are essential regardless of the team’s prior experience. They ensure a consistent, systematic approach to improvement.

They are sequential steps that must be completed in order before proceeding to the PDSA cycle: This is incorrect. The three questions are not rigidly sequential but are interconnected and iterative. Teams may revisit these questions as they progress through the PDSA cycles.

They focus primarily on resource allocation for implementing changes: This is incorrect. While resource allocation may be considered when planning changes, it is not the primary focus of the 'Three Fundamental Questions', which centre on aims, measurement, and change strategies.
