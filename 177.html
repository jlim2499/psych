Select the study design used in each of the following

205.A researcher uses a questionnaire to investigate the link between smoking and depression. She gives the questionnaire to a group of patients who all have depression. The questionnaire simply asks 'are you a smoker?
Analytical cross-sectional study This is an example of an analytical cross-sectional study. This type of study cannot lead to conclusions about causation as one would not be sure if the risk factor (smoking) preceded or followed the outcome (depression). However if very high rates of smoking were found in people with depression it may warrant further study in this area.


206.A researcher collects information on a small number of patients who have all been regular users of a new designer drug nothing of which is known. The researcher looks in detail at effects on mental and physical health.
Case series This is an example of a case series. A case series is a descriptive (observational) study on a small group looking for an association between an exposure and an outcome.


207.A research team interviews people with antisocial personality disorder and a group of controls and asks questions on a number of suspected risk factors such as physical abuse and substance misuse
Case-control study This is an example of a case-control study. The study begins with outcomes (ASPD) and then looks for historical risk factors.
