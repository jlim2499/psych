Which of the following is a 19 item checklist that authors need to consider when writing articles that describe formal studies of quality improvement?

SQUIRE

Explanation for the Correct Answer:

SQUIRE (Standards for QUality Improvement Reporting Excellence) is the correct option. SQUIRE guidelines provide a framework for reporting new knowledge about how to improve healthcare. The 19-item checklist is designed to ensure that all aspects of quality improvement are thoroughly and transparently conveyed.

Explanation for the Incorrect Answers:

MOOSE (Meta-analysis Of Observational Studies in Epidemiology) is intended for reporting meta-analyses of observational studies, not quality improvement studies.

STARD (Standards for Reporting of Diagnostic Accuracy Studies) is designed for reporting studies about diagnostic accuracy, not quality improvement.

PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) is used for systematic reviews and meta-analyses, not specifically for quality improvement studies.

CONSORT (Consolidated Standards of Reporting Trials) is a set of guidelines for reporting randomized controlled trials, not quality improvement studies.
