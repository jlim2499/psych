Regarding box and whisker plots, which of the following is most accurate?

In the absence of outliers, each whisker represents approximately 25% of the data

The correct answer is In the absence of outliers, each whisker represents approximately 25% of the data. When no outliers are present, the whiskers typically extend from the quartiles (Q1 and Q3) to the minimum and maximum values, meaning that the whiskers contain approximately 25% of the data on either side of the box (between Q1 and the minimum, and Q3 and the maximum). This is important in clinical settings where outlier detection can help identify rare but potentially critical cases, such as extreme lab values in patient cohorts.

Unlike the mean, which can be skewed by extreme values, the quartiles provide a more reliable measure of data spread in the presence of outliers, such as in clinical cases where a few extreme results might otherwise skew the overall analysis.
