In a qualitative study on women who self-harm, 7 subjects are recruited with the following ages, 18, 22, 40, 17, 23, 18, and 44. Calculate the mean age of women in the study.

26

The correct answer is 26. The mean age is calculated by adding up all the ages and then dividing by the number of subjects. In this case, if you add 18, 22, 40, 17, 23, 18 and 44 together you get a total of 182. Then divide that by the number of subjects which is seven, and you get approximately 26.

18: This appears to be a common age amongst the subjects (with two participants being this age), however it doesn't take into account any of the other ages in the study. The mean requires all data points to be considered.

31: This value seems to have been influenced heavily by the older ages in the study. While there are women in their forties participating in this study, there are also several significantly younger women whose ages need to be factored into calculating an accurate mean.

23: This could potentially be mistaken as a median rather than a mean. The median is found by arranging values from lowest to highest and choosing the middle one. However, we are asked for the mean here not median.
