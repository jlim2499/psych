The following ROC curves were produced from a study which investigated models for predicting mild cognitive impairment (MCI) using eye movement (EM) data.


Based on the graph, answer the following.

279.Which is the most accurate model?
Demo, MMSE & EM The correct answer is Demo, MMSE & EM. The model that combines demographic information (Demo), Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE) scores, and eye movement (EM) metrics has the highest area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUROC) of 0.840.


280.Which is the least accurate model?
Demo The correct answer is Demo. The model using only demographic information has the lowest area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUROC) of 0.656. This indicates it is the least accurate in predicting mild cognitive impairment (MCI).


281.What is the sensitivity for the ideal cut off for the Demo, MMSE & EM model?
80% The correct answer is 80%. The sensitivity, or true positive rate, at the ideal cut-off for the Demo, MMSE & EM model is approximately 80%. This indicates that the model correctly identifies 80% of the patients with mild cognitive impairment (MCI) at this cut-off point.

To deduce this, we refer to the ROC curve for the Demo, MMSE & EM model, which is depicted in dark blue on the graph. The ideal cut-off point is typically the point on the ROC curve that is closest to the top left corner (0,1) of the graph. This point represents the best trade-off between sensitivity and specificity, aiming to maximise the true positive rate while minimising the false positive rate.
