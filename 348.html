A study aims to compare the proportion of people who develop agranulocytosis during treatment with clozapine versus those who develop it during treatment with olanzapine. The data collected is categorical (developed agranulocytosis: yes/no) for both groups. Which statistical test is most appropriate to determine whether there is a significant difference in the proportions between the two groups?

Chi-squared test

The correct answer is Chi-squared test. The chi-squared test is used to evaluate the relationship between two categorical variables. In this case, the test compares the proportion of patients who develop agranulocytosis (yes/no) between the two treatment groups (clozapine vs olanzapine) to determine if there is a statistically significant difference.

The 5 Steps:

1) Define the research question: Is there a significant difference in the proportion of people who develop agranulocytosis between those treated with clozapine and those treated with olanzapine?
2) Establish the number of groups and their relationship: Two independent groups: patients on clozapine and patients on olanzapine.
3) Identify the variables and data types: Independent variable: Treatment group (clozapine vs olanzapine), categorical.
Dependent variable: Agranulocytosis status (yes/no), categorical.
4) Determine whether to use a parametric or non-parametric test: Since the data is categorical, a non-parametric test (chi-squared) is appropriate.
5) Select the appropriate statistical test: The chi-squared test is the correct choice for analysing differences in proportions between the two independent groups.
