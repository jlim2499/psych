A study is designed to assess a new proton pump inhibitor (PPI) in elderly patients who are taking aspirin. The new PPI is given to 120 patients whilst a control group of 240 is given the standard PPI. Over a five year period 24 of the group receiving the new PPI had an upper GI bleed compared to 60 who received the standard PPI. What is the absolute risk reduction?

5%

ARR = CER - EER
The correct answer is 5%. This represents the absolute risk reduction (ARR), calculated by subtracting the risk in the intervention group from the risk in the control group.

Here's the breakdown:

In the new PPI group, 24 out of 120 patients experienced an upper GI bleed, which gives an event rate of 24/120 = 0.2 (or 20%).
In the control group, 60 out of 240 patients experienced an upper GI bleed, giving an event rate of 60/240 = 0.25 (or 25%).

ARR = Control Event Rate (CER) - Experimental Event Rate (EER)

= 0.25 - 0.2
= 0.05 (or 5%).

Thus, the absolute risk reduction is 5%.
