An example of opportunity cost in healthcare is:

Choosing between funding a new MRI machine or hiring additional staff

The correct answer is Choosing between funding a new MRI machine or hiring additional staff. Opportunity cost refers to the value of the next best alternative that is forgone when a decision is made. In this example, investing in one resource (an MRI machine) means giving up the opportunity to allocate those funds to hire additional staff. This concept is crucial in healthcare resource allocation, where budgets are finite, and trade-offs must be carefully considered.
