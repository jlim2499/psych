A study is performed to find the normal reference range for IgE levels in adults. Assuming IgE levels follow a normal distribution, what percentage of adults will have an IgE level higher than 2 standard deviations from the mean?

2.5%

The correct answer is 2.5%. In a normal distribution, approximately 95% of the data lies within two standard deviations of the mean. This means that the remaining 5% of the data lies outside this range. Since the normal distribution is symmetrical, half of this 5% will be above two standard deviations and the other half below. Therefore, the percentage of adults who will have an IgE level higher than 2 standard deviations from the mean is 2.5%.

1.25% is incorrect. This value represents half of the remaining percentage (2.5%) of one tail but does not correspond to two standard deviations above the mean.

1.96% is incorrect. This value represents the z-score for 97.5% cumulative probability but not the percentage of data outside two standard deviations.

5% is incorrect. This value represents the total percentage of data outside two standard deviations but not just the upper tail.

0.5% is incorrect. This significantly underestimates the percentage of data above two standard deviations from the mean.
