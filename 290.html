Serum potassium is measured in a 1,000 patients with anorexia nervosa. The mean potassium is 4.6 mmol/l with a standard deviation of 0.3 mmol/l. Which one of the following statements is correct?

68.3% of values lie between 4.3 and 4.9 mmol/l

The correct answer is 68.3% of values lie between 4.3 and 4.9 mmol/l. This correctly reflects the property of the normal distribution where approximately 68.3% of the data lies within 1 standard deviation (SD) of the mean. Given that the mean potassium level is 4.6 mmol/l with an SD of 0.3 mmol/l, one SD below and above the mean are 4.3 to 4.9 mmol/l.

95% of values lie between 4.5 and 4.75 mmol/l. This statement is incorrect because it misapplies the normal distribution rules. The interval given (4.5 to 4.75 mmol/l) is too narrow to include 95% of the values. For 95.4% of the values, you need to consider 2 SDs from the mean.

95.4% of values lie between 4.3 and 4.9 mmol/l. This statement is incorrect because it describes the range that includes approximately 68.3% of the values, not 95.4%. For 95.4% of the values, you need to consider 2 SDs from the mean, which would be 4.0 to 5.2 mmol/l.

99.7% of values lie between 4.0 and 5.2 mmol/l. This statement is incorrect. The range of 4.0 to 5.2 mmol/l includes approximately 95.4% of the values, not 99.7%. For 99.7% of the values, you need to consider 3 SDs from the mean, which would be 3.7 to 5.5 mmol/l.
