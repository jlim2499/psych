A follow-up study is performed looking at the height of 100 adults who were given steroids during childhood. The average height of the adults is 169cm, with a standard deviation of 16cm. What is the standard error of the mean?

1.6

Standard error of the mean = standard deviation / square root (number of patients)
The correct answer is 1.6. The standard error of the mean (SEM) is a measure of how far the sample mean of data is likely to be from the true population mean. It can be calculated using the formula SEM = SD/√n, where SD is the standard deviation and n is the number of observations. Here, the standard deviation (SD) is 16cm and there are 100 observations (n), hence SEM = 16/√100 = 16/10 = 1.6.
