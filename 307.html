A GP practice becomes overwhelmed with patients presenting to their practice with low mood. One GP does a review of the records from the previous year and discovers that for every 150 patients presenting with this symptom, 34 are judged to have had depression.

They decide to introduce a screening method that can be done by the practice nurse. A screening tool is selected that produced the following results in a study done in primary care.

Depression presentDepression absent
Screen positive6513
Screen negative2078

Based on the above information answer the following questions.

375.What is the probability of a patient presenting to the practice with low mood actually having depression?
0.23 This is the pre-test probability and is calculated by:

34/150 = 0.23 (2dp) or 23%


376.What is the likelihood of a positive result when depression is present compared to when depression is absent?
5.43 The likelihood of a positive result when depression is present compared to when depression is absent is the likelihood ratio for a positive test (LR+).

LR+ = sensitivity / (1 - specificity)

Sensitivity = TP/(TP+FN)
= 65/(65+20)
= 0.76

Specificity = TN/(FP+TN)
= 78/(13+78)
= 0.86

LR+ = sensitivity / (1 - specificity)
= 0.76/(1-0.86)
= 0.76/0.14
= 5.43
