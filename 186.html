Which of the following is true regarding descriptive and inferential statistics?

An estimate of a population mean accompanied with a confidence interval is an inferential statistic

The correct answer is: An estimate of a population mean accompanied with a confidence interval is an inferential statistic. Inferential statistics are used to make inferences or predictions about a population based on data obtained from a sample. When we calculate the mean of a sample and accompany it with a confidence interval, we are using inferential statistics. The confidence interval provides us with an estimated range of values which is likely to include the unknown population parameter.

Inferential statistics are used to summarise data obtained from a population sample is incorrect because this statement describes descriptive statistics, not inferential statistics. Descriptive statistics are used to summarise, organise and simplify data obtained from either a sample or an entire population.

Descriptive statistics involves the use of hypothesis testing is incorrect as it's actually inferential statistics that involve hypothesis testing. Hypothesis testing allows us to make decisions or draw conclusions about populations based on sample data.

The statement Inferential statistics are limited to samples and do not provide relevant information about the population from which the sample is drawn is also incorrect. While it's true that inferential statistics are used primarily with samples, they absolutely provide relevant information about the population - in fact, their main purpose is to allow for predictions or generalisations about the population based on sample data.

Finally, Descriptive statistics enable predictions to be made about a population from a sample is incorrect because this again describes inferential, not descriptive, statistics. Descriptive statistics summarise and describe characteristics of a dataset but do not allow for making predictions or generalising results beyond that dataset.
