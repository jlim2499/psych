Regarding the correlation coefficient, which of the following is true?

It can assume any value between -1 and 1

The correct answer is It can assume any value between -1 and 1. The correlation coefficient, often denoted as Pearson's r, measures the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables. It ranges from -1 (perfect negative correlation) to 1 (perfect positive correlation), with 0 indicating no correlation.

It is a measure of similarity between two means is incorrect because the correlation coefficient measures the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables, not the similarity between their means.

It is represented by a small c is incorrect because the correlation coefficient is typically represented by the letter r, not c.

It is stated in the same units as the dependent variable is incorrect because the correlation coefficient is a unitless measure. It is a standardized value that describes the relationship between variables regardless of their units.

It is used to demonstrate causation is incorrect because the correlation coefficient only indicates the strength and direction of a relationship between two variables. It does not imply causation; other statistical methods are required to establish a causal relationship.
